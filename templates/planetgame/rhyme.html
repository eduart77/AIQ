Rhyme poems

In the sky, bright worlds align,

Nine planets in a cosmic line.

Mercury's swift, a dance so fine,

Venus glows, in sunset's shine.


Earth, our home, in circuits we define,

Engineering marvels, sparks and vine.

Mars, the red, dreams intertwine,

Jupiter, with storms divine.


Saturn's rings, like circuits combine,

Uranus tips, a tilt we find.

Neptune's winds, engineering's sign,

Pluto's heart, so true and kind.


In each planet's orbit, a design,

Circuits hum and buzz, they bind.

With humor bright, in stars we find,

A universe of wonder, all aligned.

In a vast and starry space,

Nine planets find their place.

Mercury, swift and near,

Close to the sun's bright cheer.


Venus with clouds so dense,

A place of mystery, immense.

Earth, our home, so blue,

Life in colors bright and true.


Mars with its crimson hue,

Dreams of aliens might come true.

Jupiter, giant grand,

Stormy, regal, made of bands.


Saturn with its icy rings,

A cosmic dance that sings.


Uranus, tilting on its side,

Hidden secrets it might hide.

Neptune, so far and cold,


A distant realm, stories untold.

And Pluto, small, out there,

In our hearts, it's special, rare.


Now think of circuits, bright and grand,

In the field of dreams, we stand.

Tiny paths of silver gold,

Stories of the future, told.


Electric rivers flow with grace,

Joining pieces, they embrace.

Resistors, capacitors, and gates,

All combined, new fate awaits.


Transistors switch, on-off gleam,

Creating patterns, like a dream.

So, though the cosmos seems so vast,

Engineering links our past.


Circuits, planets, both unknown,

Brave the fears, and on we'll go.
